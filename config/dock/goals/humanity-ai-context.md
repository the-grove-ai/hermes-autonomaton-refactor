# HumanityAI Grant Submission — Goal Context

## The Forcing Function

The Grove Foundation is submitting to HumanityAI for funding by end of
summer 2026. This is the single most important milestone on the board.
Every other strategic goal either produces evidence for this application
or builds the institutional credibility required to win it.

Jim is transitioning to full-time Grove Foundation work. Funding is what
makes that sustainable.

## What We Need to Demonstrate

1. **A coherent thesis.** The Autonomaton Pattern is a published,
   peer-reviewed architectural standard for sovereign, distributed AI.
   Not a pitch — a school of thought with structural implications.

2. **A working reference implementation.** Hermes (this system's own
   codebase) IS the proof point. A functioning Autonomaton built on a
   popular open-source agent framework, demonstrating the five-stage
   pipeline, Zone Model, Sovereignty Gate, and Skill Ratchet in
   daily production use.

3. **Institutional credibility.** Three confirmed advisors with Apple
   lineage (Clement Mok, Susan Kare, Randy Wigginton). Published
   standards (GRV-001 through GRV-004). A live website at the-grove.ai.

4. **Public traction.** LinkedIn content series, Substack, and
   influencer engagement demonstrating that the thesis resonates with
   practitioners, enterprise leaders, and academics.

5. **Regulatory alignment.** The EU AI Act's high-risk requirements
   map directly onto the Autonomaton's architectural properties. The
   Brinqa engagement (via Erik Cottrell) demonstrates enterprise
   demand for this approach.

## The Grove Foundation

- 501(c)(6) AI governance standards body, Indianapolis
- Publishes open architectural standards under CC BY 4.0
- Linux Foundation operational model
- Three-act architecture: Autonomaton (node) → Trellis (domain) →
  Knowledge Commons (network)

## Application Strategy

The application narrative should frame Grove as:
- Infrastructure, not a product company
- A standards body with a working reference implementation
- Aligned with humanity-first AI governance (architecture as guarantee,
  not policy as promise)
- Already operating (published standards, live site, active advisors)
  rather than hypothetical
