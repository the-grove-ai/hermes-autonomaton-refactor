# Content Pipeline — Goal Context

## Strategic Purpose

The content pipeline builds the audience that validates the Grove thesis
before anyone has to be pitched. This is guerrilla GTM: publish the
thinking, not the code. Anyone who reads the pattern and thinks "this
could work for multi-agent systems" has independently discovered the
Grove thesis. No pitch needed.

## The LinkedIn Thinkpiece Series

Five pieces, escalating from credibility-building to thesis reveal:

### Piece 1: "The Vibe Code Reckoning" (Zone 1)

- Establishes Jim as a credible voice on engineering discipline

### Piece 2: "What the Apple Generation Knew" (Zone 1)

### Piece 4: "The Governance Problem Nobody's Solving" (Zone 2)
- Auditability by design, not as afterthought

### Piece 5: "The Autonomaton Pattern" (Zone 3)
- Launch piece, links to the full pattern document
- Direct Grove positioning
- The reveal

## Content Zone Classification

- Zone 1: Practitioner-level, no thesis. Establishes credibility.
- Zone 2: Thesis-adjacent, no product pitch. Introduces concepts.
- Zone 3: Direct Grove positioning. The reveal.

Most LinkedIn pieces are Zone 2. The series architecture moves from
Zone 1 → Zone 3 over five pieces.

## The Pattern Document

Working title: "The Autonomaton Pattern: Toward Self-Authoring Software Systems"

Structure:
1. The Problem: Reactive assistant paradigm
2. The Lineage: What the PC revolution understood
3. The Pattern: Telemetry → Recognition → Compilation → Approval → Execution
4. The Principles: DEX pillars reframed for general audience
5. Reference Schemas: JSON for telemetry, intent, skill compilation, routing
6. The Zone Model: Green / Yellow / Red
7. The Flywheel: Self-reinforcing improvement loop
8. Implications: Governance, auditability, sovereign computing

Format: 15-20 pages, CC BY 4.0, hosted on the-grove.ai + PDF
Quality gate: CTO, mid-career dev, and product leader each find
something that changes how they think.

## Voice and Register

- McKinsey-influenced: lead with "so what," active voice, no hedging
- Short declarative sentences mixed with longer analytical ones
- No marketing labels or breathless superlatives
- "Less words, more weight"
- AP style for grammar and punctuation
- Institutional "we" for Grove Foundation materials
- First-person for LinkedIn (Jim's voice)
